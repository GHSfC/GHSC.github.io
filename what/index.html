What?

What kinds of fasts are there?

It is up to everyone participating in the Global Hunger Strike for Climate to choose the type of fast that is most suitable and practical for them in view of their abilities and health needs. The fast is designed to be flexible – it can involve a week or more of absolute fasting or rolling 24-hour fasts; a full- or part-fast for only a day or few days; or other alternatives, e.g. intermittent fasting (IF) for a week or longer. Here is a description of a few of the fasting alternatives, and how they are broken down.

Please note: all fasters – but especially the one-, two-week and indefinite fasters – will need supervision and monitoring as to ensure they are getting enough water, salts, vitamins and minerals, and that their weight is maintained at normal levels.

Shorter term fasts, from one to five days, and intermittent fasting, are an important sign of solidarity for long term fasters and show support for the demand.

24 Hour Fasts

A 24 hour fast is going without food for 24 hours, where one stops eating at a particular hour on one day, and then go without food for a full 24 hours. See further details here, about the risks for younger people when going on a fast.

Intermittent Fasts

Partial or intermittent rolling fasts can also be done using a 12- to 24-hour rolling schedule that includes hours of sleep, for example fasting from 8pm at night on one day until 6pm the following evening, a pattern that can then be followed on two or more successive days, or with gaps, for example every other day.

Shorter Term Fasts, of Two to Five Days

when fasting for these periods, consuming sufficient water and taking vitamins and minerals becomes particularly important. And these shorter term fasts show an even greater solidarity for the longer term fasters

Breaking Bread

When ending a shorter term fast, the fasters may choose to synchronise the ending of their fast, where they mark the occassion with a communal ‘breaking of the bread’, by having a shared meal.

One-, two-week and indefinite Fasts

The one-week, two-week and indefinites fasts are exactly what it sounds like! No food will be consumed over the period, however plenty of water, along with daily vitamins and minerals. For these longer term fasts of beyond 10 days, it’s vitally important that the fasters follow a strict refeeding protocol, by only gradually reintroducing food - further details are described here.
