Why?

We Can Still Choose to Have a Sustainable & Prosperous Future

Mitigating climate change is a major challenge that requires coordinated efforts across various sectors and regions. Which will give our children and grandchildren a chance at having a suatainable and prosperous future:

Transition to Renewable Energy: Prioritize the development and deployment of renewable energy sources like solar, wind, hydroelectric, and geothermal. Reducing reliance on fossil fuels can significantly lower global CO2 emissions. Solar is now the cheapest form of energy in history, and the renewable energy sector offers vast opportunity for employment, and is now seeing the largest increase in jobs of all sectors of the US economy.

Energy Efficiency: Enhance energy efficiency in buildings, transportation, industries, and appliances. Retrofitting old infrastructure, adopting efficient technologies, and implementing stricter energy standards can lower energy consumption and reduce emissions.

Afforestation and Reforestation: Planting trees and restoring damaged forests can act as carbon sinks, absorbing more CO2 from the atmosphere. Forests also provide biodiversity and watershed benefits.

Electrify all Transportation: Accelerate the adoption of electric vehicles and expand public transport, cycling, and walking infrastructure.

Adopting a Plant-Rich Diet: Encourage a shift towards diets rich in plants and with reduced meat consumption. Livestock farming is a significant source of greenhouse gas emissions, and a plant-rich diet can greatly reduce an individual’s carbon footprint.

Sustainable Agriculture: Implement agricultural practices that are sustainable and carbon-efficient, such as no-till farming, crop rotation, and agroforestry. Additionally, reducing meat consumption can decrease the environmental footprint of agriculture. Improving carbon sequestration through Improved Farming methods, like agroforestry, cover cropping, and conservation tillage, that draw down atmospheric CO2 and enrich the soil.

Electrify Transportation: Promote the use of electric vehicles (EVs) and develop infrastructure like EV charging stations. Also, invest in public transit systems and promote walking and cycling in urban areas.

Waste Management: Enhance recycling and composting to reduce landfill waste, which releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Encourage waste-to-energy technologies and biogas production from organic waste.
